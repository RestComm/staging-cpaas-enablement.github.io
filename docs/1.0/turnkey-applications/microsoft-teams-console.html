Overview

Telestax Message Exchange for Microsoft Teams helps Communications Providers enable their Business Customers to send and receive SMS using Microsoft Teams and to manage and monitor usage. For more information, access the General Documentation.

This document contains information for Communications Providers regarding the Message Exchange for Microsoft Teams UI Console.

To learn more about the API check out the Microsoft Teams API Documentation.

Business Customer

Communications Providers serve Business Customers.Through their own Accounts, Communications Providers can create, manage, or delete Business Customer Sub Accounts.By setting up Sub Accounts for Business Customers, the Communications Provider can easily manage billing and invoices for a specific Business Customer.

Business Customers List

Users can view a list of all Business Customers that have been added under their organization. Business Customers can be filtered based on name.

Add Business Customer

Follow the steps below to add a Business Customer:

  • Click on the Add Enterprise button in the Enterprise list page.

  • Enter the name of the new Business Customer.

  • Enter a user name for the account. A user with this name will be created for the account. If nothing is entered, the default value will be the Admin Email Address which is mentioned in the next step.

  • Choose an administrator for this Business Customer. Enter the Admin Email Address.

  • Enter the password for the Admin. This will be set in the Restcomm core platform.

  • When you are finished, click Save.

Whenever a Communications Provider adds a new Sub Account for a Business Customer, a mandatory User must be defined.This User gets created under the Restcomm Core platform as a Sub Account of the Communications Provider Account.

The Enterprise name and the Mail ID must be unique
Password should meet below criteria:

  • At least one uppercase letter(A,B,C,D…​)

  • At least one lowercase letter(a,b,c,d,…​…​.)

  • At least one number(1,2,3,4,5.)

  • At least one special character(!,@,#,$,%,&,…​…​.)

Length of the password should be greater than equal to 8.

Manage Bot Details for Business Customer

For Microsoft Teams users to be able to receive incoming SMS on Microsoft Teams Client and to be able to send outbound SMS, the following parameters should be obtained by the Business Customers as explained in this document.

  • Application ID

  • Tenant ID

  • Client Secret

  • Bot App ID

These parameters must be defined for each of the Business Customers as mentioned below:

  • Click on the Business Customer name from the Enterprise list page.

  • Enter the Application ID, Tenant ID ,Client Secret and Bot App ID from the previous step.

  • When you are finished, click Save.

Edit Business Customer

To edit the details of a Business Customer, click on the Business Customer name from the list. The following parameters can be changed:

  • Name of the Business Customer

  • Status

  • Application ID

  • Tenant ID

  • Client Secret

  • Bot App ID

Click Save after making the appropriate changes.

Delete Business Customer

To delete a Business Customer click on the customer’s name from the list of Business Customers and click the Delete button on the bottom of the screen. Once the account is deleted it cannot be undone.

Mappings

To navigate to the page, click on the Enterprise in the top header.

Users can view a list of all enterprises that have been added under the organization as well as the number of mappings created for a specific Business Customers over a specific period of time for billing and management purposes.

Each Business Customer has a list of mappings between SMS enabled numbers and Microsoft Teams IDs.

Enterprise mapping Details

If a user clicks on a mapping associated with any Business Customer, a paginated list of mappings that belong to the Business Customer will be displayed.

Add Mapping

  • Navigate to the Add Mapping page by clicking the Add Mappings button.

  • Input the following three fields:

    • Friendly name for the mapping.

    • External address i.e. the number to be SMS enabled.

    • Microsoft Teams ID

    • Users can add single or multiple mappings under a particular Business Customer.

  • Phone numbers have to be unique across the system.

  • While adding, if all the mappings are valid then the user will see the mapping list, otherwise, the user will see the rejected mappings where users can modify the Microsoft Teams ID or number.

  • When a mapping is added, the number is provisioned on Restcomm and the number is SMS enabled by Telestax.

Edit Mapping

To edit the details of a mapping, Click on the Mappings option associated with the Business Customer’s name from the list. Click on the mapping name that you wish to edit from the available list of mappings under that Business Customer. The following parameters can be changed:

  • Name

  • Microsoft Address

Click Save after making the appropriate changes.

Delete Mapping

To delete a mapping click on the Mappings associated with the Business Customer’s name. Click on the mapping name that you wish to delete. Click the Delete button on the bottom of the screen. Once this mapping is deleted it cannot be undone.

Search

To search Business Customers, Messages and Mappings , click on Search in the top menu to navigate to the page. Then click the appropriate option to search from the side menu.

Search Business Customers

A Communications Provider can search Business Customers based on the following filter parameters:

  • Name: Display Business Customers whose name partially or fully matches the entered name.

  • Status: Display Business Customers whose status is the same as the selected status.

  • Start Date: Display Business Customers created on or after this date.

  • End Date: Display Business Customers created on or before this date.

Business Customers Details

Click on the name of a Business Customer to view the details.

Search Mappings

Search mappings based on the following filter parameters:

  • Mapping Name: Display Mappings with Name that partially or fully match with the entered Mapping Name.

  • External Address: Display Mappings with the entered External Address.

  • Microsoft Teams ID: Display Mappings with the entered Microsoft Teams ID.

  • Business Customer Name: Display Mappings that belong to the selected Business Customer.

  • Start Date: Display Users created on or after this date.

  • End Date: Display Users created on or before this date.

Search Messages

Search Messages based on the following filter parameters:

  • Start Date: Lists messages that are created on or after this date.

  • End Date: Lists messages that are created on or before this date.

  • Enterprise: Lists messages that belong to this enterprise.

  • To: Lists messages that are sent to this number or Microsoft Teams Id.

  • From: Lists messages that are sent from this number or Webex Teams Id.

  • Direction: Lists messages of particular direction (INBOUND,OUTBOUND). Inbound means messages coming from Mobile to Microsoft Teams. Outbound means message sent from Microsoft Teams to mobile.

  • Channel Status: Lists messages with this channel status.

  • Channel Error Code: Lists messages with this Channel Error Code.

Understanding the Microsoft Teams API

If you would like to explore the Microsoft Teams API, we recommend having a look at the Getting Started with the Microsoft Teams API Guide.
